# Minimal Soft Launch Legal Compliance Plan

**For 3-5 User Word-of-Mouth Launch**

## Overview

This is a practical, bare-minimum legal compliance approach for Tekton's Table's soft launch. You already have an LLC with an EIN, which provides significant protection. This plan focuses only on what's truly essential.

---

## Week 1: Absolute Essentials (Free - 1 Day)

### The Only Things You MUST Do:

#### 1. Add 3 Legal Pages
Use free templates to create:
- **Terms of Service** - Copy from similar platforms, customize basics
- **Privacy Policy** - Use termly.io or freeprivacypolicy.com (free)
- **Add footer links** - Link to both documents from your site footer

#### 2. One Key Disclaimer on Checkout
Add this to your giving/checkout pages:
> "Donations may be tax-deductible if missionary operates through a registered 501(c)(3). Platform fees apply. See missionary's tax status above."

#### 3. Email Your 3-5 Missionaries
Send a simple email:
\`\`\`
Subject: Tekton's Table Platform Agreement

Hi [Name],

Before we launch, I need to confirm a few things:

1. You understand Tekton's Table charges [X]% platform fee on donations
2. You're responsible for the content on your site
3. You agree to our Terms of Service at [link]

Please reply "YES" to confirm you understand and agree.

Thanks!
\`\`\`

**Save their email replies** - this is your proof of agreement.

**Total Cost: $0**  
**Total Time: 4-6 hours**

## What You're Protected From Right Now

✅ **LLC shields your personal assets** - biggest protection  
✅ **Stripe handles payment compliance** - PCI, fraud, KYC/AML  
✅ **Small scale = regulatory invisibility** - you're not on anyone's radar  
✅ **Known users = low fraud risk** - you're not dealing with strangers  
✅ **Tax-deductibility disclaimers** - system now shows appropriate notices

## Risks You're Accepting (Acceptable for Soft Launch)

⚠️ Incomplete legal documentation (low risk with 3-5 known users)  
⚠️ No insurance yet (add at 10 users)  
⚠️ Minimal privacy compliance (GDPR won't care about 5 users)  
⚠️ No multi-state registrations (you're too small to enforce)  
⚠️ No attorney review (add when you have revenue to justify it)

**Risk Level: LOW** - The combination of your LLC + Stripe + small user base + clear disclaimers makes this acceptable.

## Growth Milestones: Add Protection When Needed

### At 10 Users (~$2K-$3K investment)

**Add:**
- Business insurance (cyber liability + general liability)
  - Protects against data breaches and lawsuits
  - Cost: $2K-$3K/year
  - Providers: Hiscox, Next Insurance, Embroker

**That's it. Nothing else needed yet.**

---

### At 25 Users (~$3K-$5K investment)

**Add:**
- Attorney review of Terms of Service ($2K-$3K)
  - Get custom Terms tailored to your platform
  - Ask about money transmitter licensing in YOUR state only
  - Get missionary agreement template reviewed

**That's it. Nothing else needed yet.**

---

### At 50+ Users (~$10K-$30K investment)

**Now you need full compliance:**
- Multi-state money transmitter analysis ($5K-$10K)
- Charitable solicitation registrations (varies by state)
- Full legal document suite ($5K-$15K)
- Content moderation system (implement the plan we created)
- Tax compliance systems (1099-K reporting)
- Enhanced insurance coverage

See `docs/soft-launch-legal-compliance-roadmap.md` for full details.

## Why This Approach Works

### 1. You Have an LLC
- Personal assets are protected
- Business liability is separated
- This is your biggest protection

### 2. Stripe Does Heavy Lifting
- PCI compliance (credit card security)
- KYC/AML (know your customer, anti-money laundering)
- Fraud detection and prevention
- Payment dispute handling

### 3. Small Scale = Low Risk
- Regulators target large platforms
- 3-5 users won't trigger enforcement
- Word-of-mouth = low fraud risk
- Known users = manageable disputes

### 4. You Can Scale Compliance
- Add protections as you grow
- Invest legal budget when you have revenue
- No need to be "enterprise ready" on day 1

---

## Red Flags: When to Stop and Get Legal Help

🚨 **Stop and consult attorney if:**
- A user threatens to sue
- You receive a cease & desist letter
- A state regulator contacts you
- You're processing $100K+/month in donations
- You're expanding beyond word-of-mouth marketing
- A missionary is accused of fraud/misuse of funds
- You experience a data breach

## Questions to Ask Your Attorney (When You Hire One)

1. Do I need money transmitter licenses with Stripe Connect?
2. Which states require charitable solicitation registration?
3. Am I liable for missionary content on the platform?
4. Should I require 1099s for missionaries receiving payments?
5. What insurance coverage do you recommend?
6. How should I handle refund/chargeback disputes?
7. What's my liability if a missionary misuses donated funds?
