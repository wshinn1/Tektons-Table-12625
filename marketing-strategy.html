Tektons Table Marketing Strategy

Free fundraising platform for missionaries worldwide

Executive Summary

Tektons Table is a free, self-sustaining platform for missionaries to raise support through a modern, donor-friendly giving experience. The platform's unique value is zero subscription costs for missionaries, funded by optional donor tips and a transparent 3% fee.

Target Audience

Primary: Missionaries & Ministry Workers

Demographics:

  • Age: 25-55
  • Location: Global, English-speaking
  • Tech-savviness: Basic to intermediate
  • Income: Low to moderate

Pain Points:

  • Can't afford expensive platforms ($50-200/month)
  • Don't have time for complex tech setup
  • Struggle with donor communication
  • Need professional presentation on budget
  • Want to focus on ministry, not admin

Psychographics:

  • Value community recommendations
  • Trust peer referrals over ads
  • Active in missionary networks
  • Use social media moderately

Secondary: Donors/Supporters

Demographics:

  • Age: 30-70
  • Location: USA primarily
  • Tech-savviness: Basic to intermediate
  • Income: Middle to upper-middle class

Motivations:

  • Support missionaries personally known
  • Prefer transparency in giving
  • Like seeing impact and updates
  • Want easy recurring giving
  • Appreciate tax receipts and tracking

Value Proposition

For Missionaries

"Raise support without the subscription. Share updates. Build relationships. All free."

Key Benefits:

  • Zero monthly fees
  • Professional fundraising page in minutes
  • Easy content sharing with BlockNote editor
  • Email supporters automatically
  • Accept one-time and recurring donations
  • Stripe payment processing built-in

For Donors

"Support missionaries you love with a modern, transparent giving experience."

Key Benefits:

  • Secure payment processing
  • Manage recurring gifts easily
  • Receive regular updates
  • Access exclusive supporter content
  • Track giving history
  • Optional platform tip to help missionaries

SWOT Analysis

S

Strengths

  • ▪
    Zero Subscription Model: No monthly fees for missionaries (competitors charge $50-200/month)
  • ▪
    Lower Platform Fees: 3% vs competitors' 5-12% + subscriptions
  • ▪
    Mission-Specific Features: Funding goals, supporter-only content, prayer updates, newsletters + post notifications built-in
  • ▪
    Multi-Language Support: 6 languages (EN, ES, PT, FR, KO, ZH) vs competitors' English/USD only
  • ▪
    Global Payment Processing: 135+ currencies via Stripe, local payment methods
  • ▪
    Optional Tip System: Innovative revenue model that keeps platform free while sustainable
  • ▪
    Fee Coverage Option: Donors can cover Stripe fees, maximizing missionary support
  • ▪
    Enhanced CRM Dashboard: View financials, track supporters, export to QuickBooks
  • ▪
    Integration-Ready: QuickBooks, DonorElf, Planning Center via CSV/API
W

Weaknesses

  • ▪
    No Brand Recognition: New platform competing against established names like Epistle.org and DonorElf that agencies already trust
  • ▪
    No Track Record: No case studies, testimonials, or proven success stories yet to show missionaries
  • ▪
    Bootstrap Startup: Solo founder with limited resources for marketing and customer support compared to funded competitors
  • ▪
    Revenue Model Dependency: Relies on optional donor tips for sustainability; needs 40%+ adoption rate to cover operations
  • ▪
    Learning Curve for Advanced Features: While simple to start, full utilization of CRM, multi-language, and integrations requires onboarding
  • ▪
    Network Effects Disadvantage: Competitors have existing missionary networks and agency partnerships that provide instant credibility
O

Opportunities

  • ▪
    Underserved Market: 400,000+ missionaries globally, most can't afford current platforms
  • ▪
    International Expansion: Non-Western missionaries growing fastest, need affordable solutions
  • ▪
    Mission Agency Partnerships: Offer free tool to their missionaries, gain bulk adoption
  • ▪
    Church Dashboards: Track all sent missionaries in one place, white-label option
  • ▪
    API Marketplace: DonorElf, Planning Center, Salesforce integrations unlock enterprise value
  • ▪
    Global Payment Methods: Accept local payments (M-Pesa, Alipay) to reach more donors
  • ▪
    Donor Discovery: Help missionaries find new supporters who care about their region/cause
T

Threats

  • ▪
    Established Competitors: Epistle.org, Donorbox, GiveButter have years of trust and features
  • ▪
    Competitors Go Free: If they adopt tip-based model, lose competitive advantage
  • ▪
    Economic Downturn: Missionary support decreases during recessions, affecting platform revenue
  • ▪
    Payment Processing Changes: Stripe fee increases or policy changes could impact model
  • ▪
    Regulatory Compliance: International fundraising regulations vary, complex to navigate

Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1

Beta Launch (Months 1-2)

Goal: Validate product with 5-10 missionaries

1. Personal Network Launch

  • Reach out to 1 missionary you know personally
  • Offer free setup assistance
  • Gather feedback intensively
  • Document success stories

2. Missionary Facebook Groups

  • Join 5-10 groups: "Missionaries Supporting Missionaries", etc.
  • Participate genuinely for 2 weeks before mentioning platform
  • Share soft launch: "Built a free tool for missionaries"
  • Offer white-glove onboarding

3. Mission Agency Outreach

  • Email 10-15 small mission agencies
  • Offer to onboard their missionaries for free
  • Position as "help your missionaries without adding costs"
  • Ask for feedback and testimonials

Success Metrics:

  • 5-10 active missionaries
  • $10,000+ donations processed
  • 3+ testimonials collected
  • Product-market fit validation
Phase 2

Organic Growth (Months 3-6)

Goal: Reach 25-50 missionaries through referrals and content

1. Content Marketing

Blog Posts (2 per month):

  • "How to Write a Missionary Support Letter"
  • "5 Ways to Keep Donors Engaged"
  • "Complete Guide to Missionary Fundraising"
  • "How to Thank Your Donors (With Templates)"

SEO Keywords:

  • free missionary fundraising platform
  • how to raise missionary support
  • epistle.org alternative

2. Referral Program

  • Refer another missionary → Both get featured on homepage
  • Refer 3 missionaries → Premium features
  • Create shareable referral link
  • Built into platform: "Invite Another Missionary"

3. Social Media Strategy

Platform Focus: Instagram & Facebook

  • Impact Stories (40%)
  • Educational Content (30%)
  • Behind-the-Scenes (20%)
  • Community (10%)

Posting: Instagram 3x/week, Facebook 2x/week

4. Partnership Strategy

Target Organizations:

  • Missionary training programs
  • Mission agencies (YWAM, OMF, Pioneers)
  • Bible colleges with mission programs
  • Church missions pastors

Goal: Scale to 100-200 missionaries

Budget Allocation:

  • Google Ads: $200/month

2. Influencer Partnerships

Micro-Influencers (5k-50k followers):

  • Missionary bloggers

3. Email Marketing

Lead Magnet:

Email Sequences:

  • Welcome Series (5 emails)
  • Nurture Series (2x month)

4. Event Marketing
